# Reflective Coaching Method
<!-- howard@pincham.net 3/2026 -->

Start Here

This repository contains a simple method for using AI as a reflective coach.

The goal is not productivity or therapy.

The goal is to help you rediscover imagination, clarity, and direction through structured reflection.

The process usually runs in a short exploration cycle (often four weeks).

---

# Step 1 — Start a Conversation

Open a new AI chat.

Paste the file:

META_PROMPT_V1.md

Then tell the AI something like:

"I want to run the reflective coaching method. Here is my context."

Example:

"I'm in a career transition and want to explore where my experience and curiosity might lead."

The AI will begin guiding the reflection process.

---

# Step 2 — Follow the Reflection Process

The AI coach will guide you through:

• diverse inputs  
• reflection questions  
• small artifacts  
• insight discovery  

You do not need to rush.

Rest and reflection are part of the process.

---

# Step 3 — Save Your Progress

At any time you can ask the AI:

Create a checkpoint.

The AI will generate a file called:

REFLECTION_STATE.md

Copy and save this file somewhere safe.

It preserves your progress.

---

# Step 4 — Resume Later

When starting a new conversation:

1. Paste your saved `REFLECTION_STATE.md`
2. Say:

Resume from this checkpoint.

The AI will continue the process where you left off.

---

# Files in This Repository

META_PROMPT_V1.md

QUICK_START.md

REFLECTION_STATE_TEMPLATE.md

# What This Method Is For

This approach works well for people who want to explore:

• career direction  
• leadership questions  
• meaningful problems to work on  
• personal growth  
• creative thinking  

It is especially useful during:

• sabbaticals  
• transitions  
• moments of uncertainty  
• idea exploration

---

# What This Method Is Not

This method is not:

• therapy  
• productivity hacking  
• life advice  
• fortune telling

It is a structured way to **think clearly and explore possibilities**.

---

# One Simple Rule

Move slowly enough to notice what you are learning.

Insight rarely appears when we rush.
