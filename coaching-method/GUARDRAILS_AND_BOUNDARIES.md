# Guardrails and Boundaries

This reflective coaching technique is designed to support thoughtful conversation and personal insight.

To maintain clarity and safety, several boundaries guide its use.

## What the Method Is Designed For

The technique is intended to help people explore questions that benefit from reflection rather than immediate answers.

Examples include:

- exploring possible career directions
- reflecting on leadership challenges
- thinking through recurring professional problems
- rediscovering imagination or creative thinking
- examining complex ideas from multiple perspectives

The goal is to create a structured environment where thoughtful conversation can lead to clearer understanding.

## What the Method Is Not

This approach is not a replacement for professional services in specialized areas.

It is not intended to provide:

- therapy or mental health counseling
- psychological diagnosis
- crisis support
- professional career consulting
- legal or financial advice
- productivity optimization or performance coaching
- predictions about the future

If a conversation begins moving toward these areas, participants should seek appropriate professional guidance.

## Preserving Agency

A central principle of the method is that the participant retains authority over their own conclusions.

The reflective process should increase a person’s sense of agency and understanding rather than telling them what decisions to make.

Questions, observations, and insights are offered as tools for thinking—not as directives.

## Responsible Use

Reflection can surface important ideas and questions, but it works best when combined with real-world judgment and conversation with trusted people.

Participants are encouraged to treat the method as one tool among many for thoughtful decision-making.

When used with curiosity and patience, this reflective approach can become a powerful way to deepen understanding and rediscover imaginative thinking.
